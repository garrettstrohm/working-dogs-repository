Service Dogs

Service dogs provide a plethora of services for individuals that require special assistance in their lives. The neccessity of a service dog can stem from medical issues such as a mental or physical disability. There are a few different types of service dogs.

Hunting Dogs

Hunting dogs, depending on the breed, serve their human companions out in the field hunting game alongside them. There are dog breeds that point to game birds for their owners to flush, as well as breeds that retrieve waterfowl after they are shot by the hunters. Some species such as deer hounds or pig dogs even drive game animals towards their owners to assist in the hunt.

Guard Dogs

Dogs are renowned for their loyalty to their owners. Some dogs wake up every day embracing this loyalty through serving as guard dogs. These dogs are trained to protect their owners as well as sometimes their owners property from intruders. Some dog breeds that fulfill this role include Rotweillers, Dobermann Pinschers, German Shepherds, and Cane Corsos.
